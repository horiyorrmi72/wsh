What is Abuse?

Abuse is any action that intentionally harms or injures another person. It can take many forms, and understanding its types is the first step toward prevention.

Physical Abuse

Involves physical harm or injury to someone, such as hitting, slapping, or other acts of violence.

Emotional Abuse

Involves undermining an individual’s self-esteem through verbal assaults, criticism, or manipulation.

Financial Abuse

Controlling or misusing another person’s financial resources without their consent.

Sexual Abuse

Involves any unwanted sexual activity, including harassment, assault, or exploitation.

Neglect

Failing to meet the basic needs of a dependent individual, such as food, shelter, or medical care.

Digital Abuse

Using technology to harass, stalk, or intimidate someone through social media, texts, or emails.
